# RUBRIC.md — ME 4505 Final Report Rubric

## Sections

### Abstract
Describes problem, method, and key results/conclusions concisely with numerical details where appropriate.

### Problem Statement
Explains what problem was being solved and what information could be learned. What knowledge is gained by doing this measurement, and how could that knowledge be used?

### Introduction
Relevant history of this type of measurement. How the sensors work. What other researchers have found about similar problems. Any theoretical calculations (heat transfer or otherwise).

### Procedure
Written in past tense. Explains exactly what was done. If the procedure was altered from the original plan, both the original and altered procedure must be explained, along with the reason for the change.

### Results
Both graphical and tabular presentation of data. Text explaining the data and pointing out key features. Calculated results must include the equations used, clearly explained, with all variables and constants defined.

### Discussion
Error analysis and/or statistical analysis of data. What went wrong. How confident the group is in the data. Errors or extraneous variables that may have affected results.

### Conclusions
Recaps the problem and summarizes key findings in one or two concise paragraphs.

### References
IEEE-style endnotes used as in-text references. All references properly formatted with all relevant information.

## Formatting Requirements

- Must follow the provided template (font and spacing requirements).
- 20-page limit (appendices and TOC do not count toward limit).
- Figures, tables, and equations properly captioned/numbered and discussed in text.

## Organization
- Information presented in the expected section.
- Subheadings added where appropriate.
- Flow of ideas easy to follow; thought processes clear.

## Grammar
- Written in 3rd person, past tense.
- Minimal typos, spelling errors, run-on/incomplete sentences.
- Language concise and direct, not awkward or wordy.
